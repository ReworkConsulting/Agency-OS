# Rework Consulting — Services & Delivery

## Core Service Stack

Every client engagement is built around three interconnected pillars:

1. **Facebook Ads** — The primary acquisition channel
2. **Sales Automations** — GHL-powered lead nurture and conversion system
3. **Creative Production** — All ads, copy, graphics, and video scripts

These three pillars work as one system. Ads drive leads. Automations capture and nurture those leads. Creative is what makes the ads work. We don't offer one without the others.

---

## The Acquisition System

What we build for every client is an end-to-end acquisition system — not just ads. Here is what it includes:

### GHL (GoHighLevel) Setup
- Client onboarded into Rework's GHL environment
- Phone A2P verification completed
- Rework snapshot deployed, including:
  - 7-day lead nurture sequence (SMS + Email)
  - 30-day follow-up nurture sequence
  - Lead alert notifications to the client
  - Pipeline / lead management setup
  - Appointment booking flow
  - Appointment reminder sequences
  - "Hammer them" strategy — structured follow-up cadence for leads who don't respond

### Facebook Ads Setup
- Rework onboards onto the client's Facebook Ad account
- Full tracking and pixel setup
- Landing pages built (hosted in GHL)
- Facebook Lead Forms created
- All leads routed directly into GHL pipeline

### Research & Strategy (Pre-Launch)
- ICP research — who we are targeting, what they think, fear, want, and say
- Ad strategy and concept development
- Copywriting for all ad components
- This phase drives everything — if the ICP is wrong, the ads are wrong

### Creative Production
- Facebook ad copy (hook, primary text, CTA)
- Image/graphic copy for ad creatives
- Video scripts for client-facing ad content
- All creative routed through internal review before going live

### Appointment Setting (Varies by Client Deal)
- Some clients: Rework manages the setters (Rework-hired, Rework-managed)
- Other clients: Client hires setters, Rework trains and sets them up
- All setters work inside GHL — calls, follow-up, and booking managed there

## First 90 Days — Standard Onboarding Order

1. GHL onboarding and A2P verification
2. Rework snapshot deployed and configured
3. Facebook ad account access granted
4. Pixel, tracking, and attribution setup
5. ICP research conducted
6. Landing pages and lead forms built
7. Ad copy and creatives developed
8. Campaigns launched
9. Lead flow tested end-to-end (ad → form → GHL → follow-up → booking)
10. Optimization begins — ads managed based on performance, not schedule

## What "Winning" Looks Like for a Client

A client engagement is successful when:
- Consistent leads are coming in at a predictable cost per lead (CPL)
- Leads are being nurtured automatically without manual follow-up
- Appointments are being booked and showing up
- The sales team (or appointment setters) are working a full pipeline
- The client has reduced chaos and gained visibility into what's working
- Revenue is growing month over month

Long-term: the client has systems, automations, an acquisition engine, and (where applicable) a sales team — and they're moving toward becoming a household name in their market.

---

## What a Winning Facebook Ad Looks Like

A winning ad is a **proven ad** — one that generates consistent leads at a consistent and ideal CPL. Specific markers:

- High click-through rate (CTR)
- High opt-in / lead form completion rate
- Leads at or below target CPL
- Ad longevity — continues to perform over time without burning out quickly

A losing ad fails on one or more of these metrics. The goal of ad management is to find the winning ads faster and run them longer.
