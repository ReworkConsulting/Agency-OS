[HOOK]
Most local service businesses waste their first $3,000 in marketing before they figure out what actually works.

[BODY]
We skip that part.

We research your buyers, your market, and your competitors before we run a single ad. That research drives everything — copy, creative, targeting.

Our clients average 40 to 60 qualified leads in the first 90 days.

Not impressions. Not clicks. Qualified leads who answer the phone.

[CTA]
Book a call. See if we're a fit.

---
STYLE NOTES: B2B hook pattern — lead with the cost of inaction or the common mistake, not with what the agency offers. This positions the viewer as making an error before they've spoken a word, which creates urgency without pressure. "We skip that part" is a pattern interrupt — short fragment after a long setup. The proof point uses "qualified leads" specifically, not "leads" — this addresses the objection that agencies inflate lead counts with junk traffic. CTA is low-commitment: "see if we're a fit" removes the fear of a hard pitch.
