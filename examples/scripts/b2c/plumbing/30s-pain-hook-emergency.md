[HOOK]
Water damage compounds fast. Every hour you wait, the repair bill gets bigger.

[BODY]
We're a local plumbing company and we do same-day emergency service. No after-hours fee, no upselling you on stuff you don't need.

You call, we show up, we diagnose it on the spot, and we tell you the price before we touch it.

That's it.

[CTA]
Call right now. We pick up.

---
STYLE NOTES: First sentence establishes cost of delay in concrete terms — not "act fast" but "the bill gets bigger." The three-item list (call, show up, diagnose, price before touching) builds trust through process transparency. "We pick up" is the most important part of the CTA for an emergency-service audience — it answers the fear that they'll get voicemail when they're standing in a flooded kitchen.
