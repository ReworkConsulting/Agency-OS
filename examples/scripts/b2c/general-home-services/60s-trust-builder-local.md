[HOOK]
I want to tell you something that most contractors won't say.

[BODY]
We're not the right fit for everyone.

If you want the cheapest quote in town — we're probably not it. We've seen what cheap looks like after the fact, and we don't want our name on it.

What we are: local, licensed, and here in three years when something needs a follow-up. Not a national franchise with a call center. Not a guy off a Facebook ad with no reviews.

We do one thing: fix it right the first time, leave the job site cleaner than we found it, and stand behind the work.

Here's what I'd ask you to do. Don't take my word. Google us. Read the reviews. Look at the photos.

Then call.

[CTA]
No pressure. Just an honest conversation.

---
STYLE NOTES: Opens with a disqualifier — "we're not for everyone" — which paradoxically builds trust by signaling confidence. The negative framing ("Not a franchise, not a guy off a Facebook ad") is more credible than positive claims. The "Google us" CTA is counterintuitive but powerful: it turns the review research the prospect was already going to do into a deliberate sales step. Final CTA is low-commitment by design.
