Who We
Are

Rework Consulting is a full-service marketing firm for home service businesses. We don't just run ads and send a report. We get inside our clients' businesses and help them grow — however that needs to happen.

We work with contractors, HVAC companies, roofers, solar businesses, and other home service companies. Most of them are already making money. They're not startups. They're real businesses that want to scale — and they need a team they can trust to make it happen.

We keep our client count at 10 active clients max. That's on purpose. When you try to serve 50 clients, you spread thin and no one gets your best work. At 10, we can go deep. We can actually solve problems, not just manage campaigns.

10 Max Active Clients

90% Home Service Focus

What Makes Us Different

Most marketing agencies are vendors. They set up ads, put them on autopilot, and send a report at the end of the month. That's not us.

We call ourselves growth partners — not vendors. Here's what that means in practice:

What Vendors Do

Run the ads. Send a report. Move on. They stay in their lane and never ask what else is broken.

What We Do

We find the real problem. We fix it. Then we ask what's next. Sales process broke? We help fix that too.

The path we take most clients on looks like this:

  1. Build the acquisition system — ads, automations, creative, landing pages
  2. Solve the next bottleneck — could be sales, hiring, operations, anything
  3. Go deeper — expand scope, upsell the right solution, stay embedded
  4. Build toward a household name — they dominate their local market

We also take a percentage of the revenue we directly generate. This keeps us fully aligned — we only win when they win.

What
We Do

Everything we build for a client is connected. Ads drive leads. Automations catch and follow up on those leads. Creative makes the ads work. You can't have one without the others — it's one system.

Facebook Ads

Primary lead acquisition channel. We run paid ads on Facebook and Instagram to generate qualified leads for each client.

Sales Automations

GHL-powered sequences that follow up with every lead automatically — so no lead goes cold without a touchpoint.

Creative Production

All ad copy, graphics, and video scripts. Creative is what makes ads actually work — it's not an afterthought here.

The First 90 Days

Every new client goes through the same onboarding order. This is not optional — these steps are in this sequence for a reason.

  1. GHL Onboarding & A2P Verification — Client is set up in our GoHighLevel environment and their phone number is verified for SMS.
  2. Rework Snapshot Deployed — Our pre-built lead nurture system goes into their account and gets customized for them.
  3. Facebook Ad Account Access — We get access to the client's ad account, set up tracking, pixel, and attribution.
  4. ICP Research — We research who their ideal customer is before a single ad is written. This drives everything.
  5. Landing Pages & Lead Forms Built — All traffic gets a destination. No landing page = no leads.
  6. Ad Copy & Creatives Developed — Written using the ICP research. Not generic. Not guesswork.
  7. Campaigns Launched & Tested — We test the full flow (ad → form → GHL → follow-up → booking) before calling it live.
  8. Optimization Begins — We manage based on data, not schedule. Winning ads run longer; losing ads get cut fast.

What "Winning" Looks Like

  • Consistent leads coming in at a predictable cost per lead
  • Leads being nurtured automatically — no manual follow-up needed
  • Appointments booked and showing up
  • The sales team has a full pipeline to work
  • The client has less chaos and more visibility
  • Revenue growing month over month

06 — Tools & Technology

Our Tech
Stack

Every tool in our stack has a specific job. There's no overlap. Don't add new tools unless something genuinely can't be handled by what's already here.

GoHighLevel
The client execution platform. Every client gets their own GHL sub-account. This is where all leads live, all automations run, all calls happen, and all appointments get booked. Appointment setters work out of GHL daily.
Airtable
Our internal operating system. This is where we track everything internally — tasks, lead logs, client data, performance metrics, SOPs. GHL handles the client-facing side; Airtable handles our internal visibility.
Facebook Ads Mgr
The ad platform. Where we build, launch, and manage all Facebook and Instagram campaigns for every client. We access this on behalf of clients and monitor it daily.
Make
Complex automations between tools. When GHL does something and we need Airtable to know about it — or when data needs to move between systems automatically — Make handles it. Use this for anything multi-step.
Zapier
Simple one-step automations. If an integration is quick and doesn't need multiple steps, Zapier handles it. Rule of thumb: simple = Zapier, complex = Make.
Slack
Internal communication. All team communication runs through Slack. Alerts, client updates, and quick approvals all come through here too.
Google Docs
Documents and shared files. Finished deliverables (ICP docs, reports, strategy docs) live here so the whole team can access them. Also where client assets like logos and brand files are stored.
Frame.io
Video review and approval. Video editors upload cuts here. The team reviews and leaves timestamped notes. This is the handoff point between video production and approval.

Quick rule: Lead comes in → GHL. Track what happened → Airtable. Move data between tools → Make or Zapier. Create a document → Google Docs. Review a video → Frame.io. Talk to the team → Slack.

07 — Client Data & Organization

Client
Management

Every client has a folder. Every folder has the same structure. This isn't just organization — it's how our AI system knows what to do for each client.

The Client Folder

When a new client comes on board, we build their folder right away. Here's what goes in it:

  • overview.md — Business info, contact details, services, ad spend, goals
  • services.md — What they offer, pricing, service area
  • competitors.md — Known and researched competitors
  • reviews_raw.md — Raw scraped reviews from Google
  • reviews.md — Curated quotes that shape the ICP
  • icp.md — The full Ideal Customer Profile document

The ICP Document

The ICP is the most important file we make for any client. It's a deep research document that covers:

  • Who the client is and how they talk
  • Who their ideal customer is and what they think
  • What the client sells and why it works
  • How to message and position the offer
  • Where and how to reach the right customers
  • How the client's sales process works

Everything else we produce — ads, scripts, emails — is built from the ICP. If the ICP is wrong, everything downstream is wrong.

Onboarding a New Client

  1. Create their folder — Named in lowercase with hyphens. Example: clients/abc-hvac/
  2. Fill out overview.md — Every field, right from the onboarding call. Blank fields get marked TBD — never skipped.
  3. Run the ICP research workflow — This populates reviews.md and icp.md from real research data.
  4. Sync to Supabase — The platform reads from Supabase, not the files directly. After any update, run the sync script so the platform stays current.

Critical Rule

Never skip the ICP research before running ads. Never start ad copy without an ICP. The ICP research workflow must complete before any downstream work happens. This order is non-negotiable.

Key Intake Fields

Business Info We Need

  • Business name, owner name, email, phone
  • Website and Google Business Profile
  • Service area and list of services
  • Average job value and starting ad spend

Marketing Context

  • Biggest marketing challenge
  • List of competitors
  • Their description of ideal client
  • Main goal for the next 6–12 months

The Agency
OS

We've built an AI-powered system that handles the research, writing, and analysis work that used to eat up hours of the team's time. This isn't a chatbot. It's a structured operating system that knows how to do our job.

How It Works

The system is organized into three layers. Each layer has a specific job:

Layer 1: Workflows

Step-by-step instructions for each task. These are like recipes — they tell the system exactly what to do and in what order. Stored as documents the AI reads before acting.

Layer 2: The AI Agent

This is the brain. It reads the workflow, makes smart decisions, runs the right tools, and handles problems when they come up. It connects the intent to the action.

Layer 3: Tools

The actual execution — web scrapers, scripts, AI image generators. These do the work. The AI tells them what to do; they do it exactly.

The Workflows We Run

Each workflow has a specific job. They must run in the right order — you can't skip ahead:

  • Onboard Client — Parse intake info, check for gaps, build the client folder
  • Build ICP — Scrape reviews, research competitors, synthesize the ICP document
  • Generate Ads — Write Facebook ad copy using the ICP (requires ICP to exist first)
  • SEO Audit — Keyword research and site audit (requires ICP to exist first)
  • Generate Report — Monthly performance summary (requires ICP to exist first)

The dependency order is strict: Onboard Client → Build ICP → then everything else. If you skip the ICP, the downstream tools have no research to work from and the outputs are garbage. Don't skip it.

Your Role in The OS

As Ops Manager, you don't need to know how to run code. But you do need to understand what the system does so you can make sure it's being used correctly.

What to Know

What each workflow does. What order they run in. What outputs they create. When the system is working correctly vs. when something's broken.

What to Watch

Is every client synced to the platform after updates? Are workflows being followed in order? Are client folders complete and up to date?

Where We're Going

This section is about the future. What you're reading right now is the current state. This is where we're heading — and why the work we do today matters so much.

The Big Goal

Right now, the Agency OS lives in Claude Code — an AI terminal on a computer. The team interacts with it directly. That works, but it's not where we want to stay long-term.

The plan is to turn this entire system into a custom software platform — a web app that the whole team can use without needing to know how the AI works underneath.

How It Works Today

The AI is run manually through a terminal. The team needs to know the commands and workflows to use it. It works, but it requires some technical knowledge.

How It Will Work

Anyone on the team clicks a button — "Generate ICP," "Write Ads," "Run SEO Audit" — and the system does the work. No technical knowledge required.

The Modules Being Built

The platform will have separate modules, each powering a different part of the agency. Here's what's planned:

Research

Builds a full picture of the client, their market, their customers, and their competitors from raw data.

ICP

Generates detailed customer profiles and messaging insights. The engine behind everything we write.

Copywriting

Writes Facebook ads, video scripts, emails, landing pages — all from the ICP research.

SEO

Audits websites, analyzes competitor SEO, finds keyword gaps, proposes content strategies.

Creative

Generates ad concepts, creative briefs, and image prompts for all visual ad assets.

Reporting

Pulls performance data and produces clean, client-specific reports automatically.

Brand

Generates brand packs — color systems, typography, visual direction — for clients who need it.

Operations

Manages tasks, tracks accountability, sends renewal alerts, and flags anything overdue.

Client Portal

A clean dashboard where clients can see their own data, reports, and campaign status.

Why this matters for your role: The system you're operating today is the foundation for the platform we're building tomorrow. Every process you follow, every workflow you enforce — it all feeds into this. The cleaner we run it now, the smoother the platform will be when it launches.
